# Directory Websites — Revenue Research
## Compiled by Max — March 16, 2026

---

## What Is a Directory Website?
A website that lists businesses, services, or resources in a specific niche or location. Users search for what they need; listed businesses pay to be found.

Think: Angi, Yelp, Zillow, Thumbtack — but niche and owned by you.

---

## Why This Is Smart Right Now
- Niche directories are thriving in 2026 — broad directories are dying, focused ones are winning
- Low competition vs. the big players when you go narrow enough
- SEO-friendly by nature — lots of content, lots of location pages
- Semi-passive income once established
- Synergy with LeadHub: a contractor directory IS a lead gen machine

---

## Revenue Models (Ranked by Viability)

### 1. Lead Generation Fees (Best)
- Directory captures homeowner requests
- Sell the lead to listed contractors
- $15–75 per qualified lead depending on trade
- **This is literally what Angi does — and James already built the infrastructure for it**

### 2. Premium Listings / Freemium (Most Common)
- Basic listing free, premium listing $25-99/month
- Premium = featured placement, photos, reviews, call tracking
- Scales well — low overhead per listing

### 3. Sponsored/Featured Placement
- Contractor pays to appear at top of search results
- $50-200/month per sponsored slot
- Limited inventory = scarcity = higher prices

### 4. Subscription Memberships
- Contractors pay monthly to be in the directory
- $49-199/month for verified, active listing
- Recurring revenue — best for cash flow

### 5. Display Advertising
- Sell banner ads to suppliers (materials, insurance, tools)
- Only works once you have real traffic
- Don't count on this early

## Build Approach

### Option A — WordPress + Directory Plugin (Fastest)
- Plugins: Business Directory Plugin, Listify, GeoDirectory
- Can launch in a week
- $0-100/month hosting + plugin cost
- Good enough to validate the concept

### Option B — Custom Built (Better Long-Term)
- Build on top of existing LeadHub stack (React + Supabase)
- Fully controlled, integrates natively with pipeline
- Max can build this
- 2-3 weeks to MVP

### Option C — No-Code (Softr, Webflow + Airtable)
- Fastest to launch, least customizable
- Good for testing a niche before building

**Recommended path:** Start with Option A to validate (do listings sell?), then rebuild on our stack when proven.
